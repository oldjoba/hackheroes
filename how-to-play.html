🦸 What is this?

BTC Hack Heroes is a fun way to learn cybersecurity by solving in-browser challenges. You'll crack passwords, spot tricks, fix gadgets, and outsmart the bad guys — one mission at a time. Best for ages 8–14, but fun for everyone.

🎮 For Students

1
Join your class. On the home page click Join a class, type the class code your teacher gave you, pick a fun nickname, and hit join. No email or password needed!
2
Pick a challenge. Your assigned missions appear on the home page. Click one to open it.
3
Read the briefing. Every challenge starts with a short story explaining your mission and what you need to find.
4
Use the tools. Some challenges give you gadgets and tools on screen — experiment with them to uncover the answer.
5
Stuck? Use a hint. Each challenge has hints you can reveal one at a time. Try to use as few as possible — fewer hints means a higher spot on the leaderboard! 💡
6
Submit your answer. Type your answer and submit. If it's right, you'll see a 🎉 success and the challenge is marked complete.
🏆
Race to the top! Finish all your assigned challenges to climb the live leaderboard. The first hero to complete them all gets confetti and the crown 👑.

🧑‍🏫 For Teachers

1
Create an account. Go to Teacher and register with your email and a password.
2
Make a class. Give it a name. You'll get a class code and a join link to share with students.
3
Assign challenges. Tick the challenges you want your class to complete. Students only see what you assign.
4
Share the code. Give students the class code (or the join link). They join with just a nickname — no personal details, kid-safe by design.
5
Watch the live leaderboard. Open your class Leaderboard to see progress update in real time — who's solving, how many hints they've used, and who finishes first. 📊

⭐ Pro Tips

  • Read the whole briefing before you start — the clues are in there.
  • Hints cost you ranking, so think first, peek later.
  • There are no wrong attempts — keep trying until it clicks.
  • Stuck for ages? Ask a friend or your teacher. Hacking is a team sport!
